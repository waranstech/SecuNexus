Security Challenges in E-Commerce & Fintech

E-commerce and fintech companies ship fast, handle sensitive financial data, and rely on complex microservice architectures. Security must keep pace without slowing down innovation.

Rapid Deployment Cycles

E-commerce platforms deploy 10+ times per day across microservices. Traditional security scans that take hours are incompatible with this velocity. Teams bypass slow tools, leaving vulnerabilities undetected in production code.

PCI-DSS Compliance

Any application touching payment card data must meet PCI-DSS v4.0 requirements. This includes the checkout flow, payment API integrations, tokenization services, and every microservice in the payment processing chain.

DPDP Act Requirements

The Digital Personal Data Protection Act mandates strict controls over customer data processing, consent management, and breach notification. E-commerce platforms handling millions of user profiles must demonstrate compliance.

Microservices Complexity

Modern e-commerce runs on hundreds of microservices: catalog, cart, checkout, inventory, notifications, recommendations. Each service has its own codebase, dependencies, and API contracts that must be individually secured.

Third-Party Integrations

Payment gateways, logistics providers, marketing platforms, analytics services, and marketplace aggregators all connect via APIs. Each integration introduces supply chain risk and potential data exposure points.

High-Value Targets

E-commerce and fintech platforms are prime targets for account takeover, payment fraud, promotion abuse, and data theft. Attackers actively probe for vulnerabilities in authentication, authorization, and business logic.
